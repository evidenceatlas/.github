# EvidenceAtlas

EvidenceAtlas is a thesis research workspace for evidence cards, claims, and structured export.

It is built for thesis writers, PhD students, and research-masters students who already have papers, quotes, and findings, but need a cleaner way to move from reading to drafting.

**Bridge between reading and drafting**

EvidenceAtlas keeps the evidence layer structured without trying to become your citation manager or full writing environment.

It replaces scattered note fragments, spreadsheet tracking, and ad hoc source-to-quote workflows inside a project. It does not replace Zotero, your reference manager, or your main writing tool like Word, Google Docs, or LaTeX.

## Core workflow

1. **Add sources**  
   Capture papers, reports, webpages, and thesis references without replacing your reference manager.
2. **Create evidence cards**  
   Store the excerpt, summary, interpretation, section, status, tags, and source in one compact record.
3. **Export for drafting**  
   Filter what matters and generate structured Markdown or CSV for literature reviews, chapter sections, and dissertation drafting.

## What shapes the product

- **Project-first organisation** for thesis chapters, study streams, and literature reviews
- **Structured evidence cards** that keep research synthesis clean and portable
- **Claims and writing sections** that help turn evidence into arguments
- **Export by design** so research can move cleanly into drafting workflows

## In one line

Sources -> evidence cards -> claims -> structured writing handoff
